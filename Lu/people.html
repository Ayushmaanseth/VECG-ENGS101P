[1] (MORTON HEILIG : THE GENIUS WHO MADE A WORKING VR MACHINE IN 1957 2016)

Morton Heilig

Born : 22 December, 1926
Died : 14 May, 1997

Morton was a pioneer in VR technology, he was called the "Father of Virtual Reality" in various articles and researches. He is also a philosopher, inventor and filmmaker at the same time!

He applied his cinematographer experience and developed the Sensorama with the help of his partner in 1962


[2] (Sensorama 2017)

Sensorama is a private cinema which the viewer is able to watch the movie that they are able to smell, see, touch and hear.

  • But sadly the process of filmmaking cost a lot of money and business communities couldn't find a way to sell it

    [3] (Scott Fisher (techonologist) 2017)

    Scott Fischer

    Born : 1951
    still alive

    1985 - 1990

    Scott found the Virtual Environment Workstation Project for NASA and he was the director for it.

  • they developped a simulator which is able to rehearse for the space station maintenance

    • 1990

    Scott founded the Telepresence Research with Brenda Laurel, which was one of his partner when he was working for Atari.

  • it is a company which specializes in first-person media and virtual reality.

    • He was also staying in the Graduate School of Media and Governance at Keio University as a Project Professor.

  • he led project which explores augmented reality, which the users is able to make changes and view location-based data base on physical world.

    • 2001

    Scott moved to the University of Southern California.

    He was working in the division that focused on mediums of immersive mobile and video games and he created the research initiatives.

  • He also chaired for the division for 10 years and left at 2011.

    Nonny de la Pena

    Birth: unknown
    still alive

    Nonny is wildly regarded as the "Godmother of virtual reality"
    She is also the founder and CEO of the Emblematic Group.
    Using the combinition of Unity graphics and real witness audio, she recreated VR video based on real life example.

    2007

    She worked with Peggy Weil who is a digital media artist, and they took a part of 2004 Pyedog documentary 'Unconstitutional: The War on Our Cilvil Liberties' and made an immersive environment named 'Gone Gitmo'.

    2009

    Nonny began working with virtual reality headsets and experimented around it.

    2010

    She began to create the 'Hunger in Los Angeles' which is an VR documentary that focus on the starving problem happening in Los Angeles area.

    With the help of her intern Michaela Kobsa-Mark and USC's Mixed Reality Lab, she built the VR pieces.

    [5] (Brenda Laurel 2017)

    Brenda Laurel

    Birth: 20 November 1950
    still alive

    Brenda is famous in VR industry by her help in terms of theory of interactivity.

    1979 - 1983

    Brenda started her career at Atari, she was a software strategist at first then she became a research staff at the Atari Systems Research Laboratory she worked on the theory of interactive environments that is related to first person presence.

    1993

    Having the support by Interval Research, Brenda ran the Placeholder virtual reality project at Banff Centre for the Arts.

  • Placeholder was a VR project which discovered a new model for multi-person narrative action in VR condition.

    Ivan Sutherland

    Birth: 16 May, 1938
    still alive

    Ivan is wildly regarded as the "Father of Computer Graphics"

    1968

    He created 'The Sword of Damocles', which is the first VR and AR head-mounted display system with Bob Sproull (his student).

    1996

    He won the CyberEdge Journal Virtual Reality Pioneer Award.
